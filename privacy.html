Privacy Policy

ToolHub is built to be privacy-friendly. Many tools run directly in your browser so your files stay on your device. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, what we don't collect, and how ads/analytics may work on this site.

Summary

  • No account required: ToolHub does not require signups.
  • Local processing: for tools like image compression, processing happens in your browser.
  • Limited data collection: we may collect basic usage analytics to improve the site.
  • Ads: we may show ads to keep tools free.

Information we collect

ToolHub may collect limited non-personal information such as:

  • Approximate location (country/region)
  • Device/browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referral sources (e.g., search engines)

This data helps us improve the performance and usefulness of our tools.

Information we do NOT collect

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal information such as passwords, banking details, or government IDs.

ToolHub does not require user accounts, so we generally do not store names, emails, or profiles unless you contact us directly.

Files and tool inputs

Some tools, such as the image compressor, can run fully in your browser. When a tool runs locally:

  • Your files are processed on your device.
  • Your files are not uploaded to our servers.
  • Closing the page clears tool session data.

If any future tool requires server processing, we will clearly state it on the tool page.

Cookies

ToolHub may use cookies to improve user experience, support analytics, and enable ads. Cookies are small files stored on your device to remember preferences and measure usage.

Advertising (Google AdSense)

ToolHub may display ads served by Google and other partners. These providers may use cookies or device identifiers to show personalized or non-personalized ads based on your settings and local regulations.

Google may use the DoubleClick cookie to enable interest-based advertising. Users can control ad personalization through their Google Ads settings.

Third-party services

We may use third-party services for:

  • Analytics (traffic measurement)
  • Advertising (ad delivery + fraud prevention)
  • Hosting (Netlify / CDN)

These services may process limited technical information to provide their functions.

Data retention

We retain analytics data only as long as needed for site improvement and security monitoring.
